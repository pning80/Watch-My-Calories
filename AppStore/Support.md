## Troubleshooting

### Food analysis isn't working
- Ensure your device has an active internet connection. Food photos are sent to a server for AI analysis and require connectivity.
- Try taking a clearer, well-lit photo of your food.
- Make sure AI consent is enabled in **Settings > Privacy**. If you declined AI consent during onboarding or later toggled it off, food analysis will be disabled.

### No food detected
- If the app can't identify food in your photo, you'll see a "No Food Detected" message. This is different from a connection error — it means the AI didn't recognize any food items.
- Try retaking the photo with better lighting, a closer angle, or with food items more clearly visible.
- You can also log food manually using the **+** button on the Dashboard.

### Camera permission denied
- Go to **Settings > Watch My Calories > Camera** on your device and enable camera access.
- Alternatively, you can analyze food from an existing photo by tapping **+** on the Dashboard and choosing **Choose from Photo Library**.

### HealthKit data not showing
- Go to **Settings > Health > Data Access & Devices > Watch My Calories** and ensure Active Energy is enabled.
- HealthKit is optional — the app works without it, but your calorie goal won't adjust for activity.

### Ads
- Watch My Calories is free and supported by ads. You may see banner ads on some screens and a native ad during food analysis.
- All ads are non-personalized. No personal data from the app (food logs, profile, photos) is collected or shared for advertising purposes.

### Calorie goal seems wrong
- Your daily calorie goal is calculated from your profile data (age, height, weight, sex, and activity level) using the Mifflin-St Jeor formula. Update your profile in **Settings** if any values are incorrect, then tap **Calculate Recommended Goal** under **Settings > Daily Goals** to recalculate.
- You can also set a custom calorie goal by editing the **Target Calories** field directly under **Settings > Daily Goals**.
- If HealthKit is enabled, your goal adjusts upward based on active calories burned throughout the day.

### Changing units
- Go to **Settings > Unit System** to switch between US Customary and Metric. This affects how food quantities and body measurements are displayed.

### Logging food manually
- Tap the **+** button on the Dashboard to add a food entry without using the camera.
- You can also choose **Choose from Photo Library** to analyze an existing photo for calories.

### Deleting your data
- Delete individual food entries or entire meal groups from within the app.
- To remove all data, uninstall the app. All personal data (food logs, photos, profile) is stored on your device only. The only data stored remotely is anonymous device security keys used for app integrity verification — no personal information is kept on our servers.
