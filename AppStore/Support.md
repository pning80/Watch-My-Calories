## Getting Started

### Logging Food
Tap the **Log Food** tab to open the logging sheet with three options:
- **Scan Food** — Take a photo of your meal for AI-powered calorie estimation.
- **Choose from Library** — Select an existing photo from your photo library.
- **Log Manually** — Enter food name, quantity, and calories by hand.

### Scanning Menus
Tap the **Scan Menu** tab to analyze a restaurant menu:
- **Scan Menu** — Photograph a restaurant menu with your camera.
- **Choose from Library** — Select an existing menu photo.
- **Stored Menus** — Browse previously scanned menus.

The AI identifies menu items and estimates calories and macronutrients for each. Tap any item to expand its nutritional breakdown. Successfully analyzed menus are saved automatically and can be accessed later via **Stored Menus**.

### Settings & About
Tap the **menu button** (...) in the top corner of the Dashboard or History screen to access **Settings** and **About**.

## Troubleshooting

### Food analysis isn't working
- Ensure your device has an active internet connection. Food photos are sent to a server for AI analysis and require connectivity.
- Try taking a clearer, well-lit photo of your food.
- Make sure AI consent is enabled in **Menu (...) > Settings > Privacy**. If you declined AI consent during onboarding or later toggled it off, food analysis will be disabled.

### No food detected
- If the app can't identify food in your photo, you'll see a "No Food Detected" message. This is different from a connection error — it means the AI didn't recognize any food items.
- Try retaking the photo with better lighting, a closer angle, or with food items more clearly visible.
- You can also log food manually using the **Log Food** tab and choosing **Log Manually**.

### "Not a Menu"
- When scanning a menu, the AI determined the photo is not a restaurant menu.
- Ensure the photo clearly shows menu items with names and descriptions.
- Make sure the image is not blurry, cropped, or partially obscured.

### Camera permission denied
- Go to **Settings > Watch My Calories > Camera** on your device and enable camera access.
- Alternatively, you can analyze food from an existing photo by choosing **Choose from Library** from the Log Food or Scan Menu sheet.

### Location access
- The app may request location access when scanning menus. Your approximate location helps the AI identify the restaurant's cuisine for more accurate calorie estimates.
- Location is **optional** — menu scanning works without it.
- Location is **not stored** — it is used only during the analysis request.
- To grant or revoke: go to your device's **Settings > Watch My Calories > Location**.

### HealthKit data not showing
- Go to **Settings > Health > Data Access & Devices > Watch My Calories** and ensure Active Energy is enabled.
- HealthKit is optional — the app works without it, but your calorie goal won't adjust for activity.

### Ads
- Watch My Calories is free and supported by ads. You may see banner ads on some screens and a native ad during food analysis.
- All ads are non-personalized. No personal data from the app (food logs, profile, photos) is collected or shared for advertising purposes.

### Calorie goal seems wrong
- Your daily calorie goal is calculated from your profile data (age, height, weight, sex, and activity level) using the Mifflin-St Jeor formula. Update your profile in **Menu (...) > Settings**, then tap **Calculate Recommended Goal** under **Daily Goals** to recalculate.
- You can also set a custom calorie goal by editing the **Target Calories** field directly under **Daily Goals**.
- If HealthKit is enabled, your goal adjusts upward based on active calories burned throughout the day.

### Changing units
- Go to **Menu (...) > Settings > Unit System** to switch between US Customary and Metric. This affects how food quantities and body measurements are displayed.

### Logging food manually
- Tap the **Log Food** tab and choose **Log Manually** to add a food entry without using the camera.
- You can also choose **Choose from Library** to analyze an existing photo for calories.

### Deleting your data
- Delete individual food entries or entire meal groups from within the app.
- Delete stored menu scans from the **Stored Menus** screen.
- To remove all data, uninstall the app. All personal data (food logs, photos, menus, profile) is stored on your device only. The only data stored remotely is anonymous device security keys used for app integrity verification — no personal information is kept on our servers.
